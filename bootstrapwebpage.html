  About

The National Defense Academy (NDA) was established in 1954 to provide common military training to cadets of the Indian Army, Indian Navy, and Indian Air Force. Field Marshal KM Cariappa, the first Indian commander-in-chief of the Indian Armed Forces, in the early 1950s first proposed the idea for the NDA.
The NDA was established in response to the needs of the newly independent Indian nation, which was facing security challenges on multiple fronts. The academy was intended to serve as a single training institution for the three services, providing a common foundation of military education and training to all cadets before they went on to specialized training at their respective service academies.
